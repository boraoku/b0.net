Section 02

Getting around

Your hotel sits on the edge of the old city, so most landmarks are an easy walk; the conference is across the Danube and best reached by U-Bahn.

Hotel → the conference

From Wien Mitte (under the hotel): U4 → Schwedenplatz, change to U1 → Kaisermühlen–Vienna International Centre, then a 5-min walk. ≈15–20 min.

Buy a Wiener Linien 24/48/72-hour or weekly pass — it covers U-Bahn, trams and buses. The CAT and S-Bahn to the airport also leave from Wien Mitte.

Section 04

Classical music & opera

The opera season runs to the end of June, so there's something every night — and the once-a-year highlight falls on one of your free evenings.

★ The headline event

Friday 19 June — Summer Night Concert at Schönbrunn

The Vienna Philharmonic plays a free open-air concert in the floodlit palace gardens, conducted by Lorenzo Viotti with Welsh bass-baritone Sir Bryn Terfel. No ticket needed; gates open around 17:00 and the music begins about 20:45. Arrive early, bring something to sit on, and pair it with a daytime visit to the palace.

Flexible, no-booking options every night

If you'd rather decide on the day, the Kursalon (5 min away) and the Vienna Mozart Orchestra run nightly Strauss & Mozart concerts (~20:15), and there are candle-lit Vivaldi Four Seasons programmes in Karlskirche and St. Stephen's.

Tickets: opera at wiener-staatsoper.at; Musikverein at musikverein.at; Konzerthaus at konzerthaus.at. Dress is smart-casual; the State Opera also sells inexpensive standing-room tickets on the day.

Section 05

After dark: jazz, rock & cabaret

Beyond the concert halls, Vienna has a deep contemporary scene. A few real options during your week — louder, later and more unexpected.

19
Jazz
Porgy & Bess
Riemergasse 11, 1st district · ≈15 min walk or U3 to Stubentor

Vienna's premier jazz club since 1993 — intimate and serious, around 70,000 guests a year, with programming that runs from straight-ahead jazz to electronic and world music. Standing and seated tickets; arrive early for a spot near the bar.

Sat 13 JunJazzorchester Vorarlberg 'Encore' feat. Phoebe Violet Quartet
Sun 14 JunLakecia Benjamin 'We Dream' (USA) — fiery, post-Coltrane saxophone
Tue 16 JunMUK.jazz.festival — young Vienna-conservatory talent
Thu 18 JunOpen Jazz Vienna club night — genre-crossing premieres
20 · 21
Rock, indie & metal
Gasometer · Arena Wien
Both southeast of the centre · U3 to Gasometer, or tram to Arena

Vienna's two great contemporary-music venues: the Gasometer, a vast converted Victorian gas-holder, and Arena Wien, a beloved indoor/open-air complex in a former slaughterhouse.

Mon 15 JunThe Flaming Lips — psychedelic-rock spectacle · Gasometer
Mon 15 JunBilly Talent — Canadian punk-rock · Arena Wien
Tue 16 JunMegadeth + Anthrax — thrash-metal legends · Gasometer
Tue 16 JunEmpire of the Sun — electro-pop, huge visual show · Arena Open Air
Sat 20 JunOf Monsters and Men — Icelandic indie-folk · Gasometer
22
Cabaret & burlesque
Mirage — Die Eventlocation im Prater
Prater 75, 2nd district · beside the Ferris wheel, ≈15 min by tram/U-Bahn

A glamorous burlesque dinner-show: around 20 international performers across three acts of burlesque, circus and live music, with a four-course gala dinner and an English-speaking host. Red velvet, chandeliers and an intimate room — sensual and witty rather than seedy. Doors 18:30, show ~19:30, about three hours.

Select evenings'Burlesque Spectacular' — check the date selector when booking (often mid-week and weekends)

Tickets via each venue: porgy.at, gasometer.at / arena.wien, and mirage.wien. Line-ups a year out can shift — confirm dates before booking.

Section 06

The great museums

Numbered 5–8 to match the map — the four collections most worth your wife's days.

5
Kunsthistorisches Museum
World-class Old Masters: Bruegel (the world's largest collection), Vermeer, Caravaggio, Velázquez — in a palace of a building. Allow half a day.
🕐 Daily 10:00–18:00 (Thu to 21:00)🚶 ≈20 min walk / U-Bahn to Museumsquartier
The café under the cupola is a destination in itself.
6
Belvedere (Upper)
Home of Klimt's The Kiss and the largest Klimt collection anywhere, in a baroque palace with formal gardens.
🕐 Daily 09:00–18:00🚶 ≈15–18 min walk
Go early to beat the crowd around The Kiss.
7
Albertina
Monet to Picasso in the Batliner collection, plus Dürer's prints and grand Habsburg state rooms.
🕐 Daily 10:00–18:00 (Wed & Fri to 21:00)🚶 ≈15 min walk
Late Wednesday opening pairs well with a conference-day evening.
8
Leopold Museum
The definitive Egon Schiele collection and Vienna 1900 — Klimt, design, Secessionist art — in the MuseumsQuartier.
🕐 Daily exc. Tue, 10:00–18:00🚶 ≈20 min walk / U2
Closed Tuesdays — plan around 16 Jun.
Section 07

Landmarks & neighbourhoods

Numbered 9–18 to match the map — palaces, churches, markets and the playful corners of the city.

9
St. Stephen's Cathedral
Vienna's Gothic heart, with its patterned tiled roof. Climb the South Tower or take the lift up the North Tower for city views.
🕐 Tourist hours vary; tower climbs daily🚶 ≈15 min walk / U3
The catacombs are by guided tour.
10
Hofburg Palace
The Habsburgs' winter residence — Imperial Apartments, the Sisi Museum and the Silver Collection.
🕐 Imperial Apartments & Sisi Museum daily 09:00–18:00🚶 ≈18 min walk
Buy the combined Sisi ticket.
11
Schönbrunn Palace
The imperial summer palace and UNESCO gardens. Tour the state rooms, then climb to the Gloriette for the view — and on 19 June, stay for the Summer Night Concert.
🕐 Daily 08:30–17:30; park free🚶 U4 direct from Stadtpark (≈25 min)
Book a timed palace ticket in advance.
12
Secession
The golden-domed manifesto of Viennese Art Nouveau, housing Klimt's Beethoven Frieze (bring/borrow headphones for the music).
🕐 Daily exc. Mon, 10:00–18:00🚶 ≈15 min walk
Closed Mondays — plan around 15 Jun.
13
Spanish Riding School
The famous Lipizzaner stallions in the Hofburg's baroque Winter Riding School. Watch a performance or the cheaper morning exercise.
🕐 Morning training & scheduled performances🚶 ≈18 min walk
Check the performance calendar; book ahead.
14
Karlskirche
A baroque masterpiece; a panoramic lift rises into the dome for a close look at the frescoes. Vivaldi concerts most evenings.
🕐 Mon–Sat from 07:30/08:30; Sun pm🚶 ≈12 min walk
Reflecting pool out front for photos.
15
Giant Ferris Wheel · Prater
The 1897 Riesenrad and the sprawling Prater funfair — fun in the late afternoon or after dinner; great views of the city.
🕐 Daily ~09:00–23:45🚶 ≈15 min by tram/U-Bahn
Across the Danube Canal in the green Prater (next to the Mirage show).
16
Hundertwasserhaus
Friedensreich Hundertwasser's playful, undulating, tree-sprouting apartment block — a complete antidote to imperial Vienna.
🕐 Exterior viewable anytime🚶 ≈12 min walk / tram
Residential — admire from outside; the Village shop is opposite.
17
Naschmarkt
Vienna's liveliest market — meze, cheeses, spices, wine bars and brunch spots. A great lunch stop.
🕐 Mon–Sat (busiest Sat); closed Sun🚶 ≈18 min walk / U4
Saturday adds a big flea market.
18
Stadtpark · Johann Strauss Monument
The golden statue of the Waltz King, right outside your hotel — the most Viennese photo you'll take all week.
🕐 Open daily🚶 At your doorstep
Lovely for a morning coffee stroll.
Section 08

A suggested rhythm

Only a sketch — rearrange freely. It avoids the two midweek closures (Leopold shut Tue 16, Secession shut Mon 15) and saves the big free evening for Schönbrunn.

Sun 14 — Belvedere & gardens
Klimt's The Kiss, then the baroque park. Evening: Musikverein, or Lakecia Benjamin's jazz at Porgy & Bess.
Mon 15 — Kunsthistorisches Museum + Hofburg/Sisi
Old Masters in the morning, imperial apartments after. Evening: Così fan tutte — or The Flaming Lips at the Gasometer.
Tue 16 — Albertina + St. Stephen's + Naschmarkt lunch
(Leopold is closed today.) Evening: Animal Farm, or Megadeth + Anthrax for the brave.
Wed 17 — MuseumsQuartier & the Leopold; Secession
Vienna 1900 and Schiele. Evening: Così fan tutte, the Albertina's late hours, or the Mirage burlesque show.
Thu 18 — Schönbrunn Palace & Gloriette
A half-day at the summer palace. Evening: Animal Farm, or jazz at Porgy & Bess.
Fri 19 — free · the headline day
A relaxed morning, the palace or a museum, then the Summer Night Concert at Schönbrunn.
Sat 20 — free · old-town finale
St. Stephen's, the Hofburg, the Spanish Riding School and the lanes off Kärntner Straße; Naschmarkt for lunch. Evening: Manon ballet, or Of Monsters and Men at the Gasometer.
